## 4.2 Users

SUMMS serves a diverse set of users, each interacting with the system in different ways:

- Citizens and commuters who use shared vehicles, parking facilities, and public transport.
- Mobility service providers, such as bike-sharing, car-sharing, and scooter operators.
- Public transportation operators managing buses, metros, or trams.
- City administrators responsible for urban mobility planning and regulation.
- System administrators who manage and maintain the platform.

## 4.4 Outcome of the Project

The implementation of SUMMS is expected to deliver a more efficient, sustainable, and user-centered urban mobility ecosystem. By improving coordination among transportation services, SUMMS can reduce traffic congestion, lower emissions, and enhance the quality of life in smart cities. The system also provides a scalable foundation for future mobility innovations and smart city initiatives.

## 4.5 Core Features of the Project

### 1. Shared Mobility Management
Integration of bike-sharing, car-sharing, and scooter services, allowing users to locate, reserve, and access vehicles through a unified interface.

### 2. Parking Management
Real-time monitoring of parking availability, pricing, and reservations to reduce search time and congestion.

### 3. Public Transportation Coordination
Integration with public transit schedules, delays, and capacity data to improve route planning and service reliability.

### 4. Real-Time Analytics and Monitoring
Collection and analysis of mobility data to support traffic optimization and long-term urban planning.

**Examples of expected analytics:**
- Number of bicycles currently rented
- Number of scooters currently available
- Number of parking spots reserved per city
- Total trips completed today
- Most used mobility option (bike vs scooter)
- Usage per city (e.g., Montreal vs Laval)

### 5. User Information Services
Personalized travel recommendations based on preferences, location, and real-time conditions.

**Examples:**
- Preferred city
- Preferred mobility type (bike or scooter)

**Example scenario:**
If the user prefers scooters, the dashboard may display:
> “Scooters are currently available in Montreal. You may reserve one now.”

### 6. Administrative Dashboards
Tools for city officials to monitor system performance, enforce policies, and evaluate sustainability goals.

**Examples of expected admin information:**
- Total number of registered users
- Active rentals by city
- Parking utilization per city
- Number of completed trips
- Usage comparison between bikes and scooters
